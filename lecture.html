All you need to know about Cellular Respiration

The chemical equation of Cellular Respiration.

DID YOU KNOW?


Your body produces and uses up the equivalent of its own weight in ATP every day!

Cellular respiration is essential for life because it gives cells the energy they need to do everything they do. It turns food (like glucose) into energy (ATP), which powers all the activities in our cells, like moving, growing, and repairing. Without this energy, cells couldn’t survive or function properly.

The 2 types of Cellular Respiration

Aerobic Respiration

A type of cellular respiration that happens in the presence of oxygen. It is the process by which cells convert glucose into energy, with oxygen being used in the process.

Anaerobic Respiration

A type of cellular respiration that occurs without oxygen. In this process, cells still break down glucose to produce energy (ATP), but because oxygen isn't available, it produces less energy compared to aerobic respiration.

The Main Stages of Cellular Respiration

GLYCOLYSIS

(Cytoplasm)

  • Process: Breakdown of Glucose into pyruvate.
  • Key outputs: 2 ATP, 2 NADH
  • Fun fact: This occurs in both types of cellular respiration

KREBS CYCLE

(Mitochondrial Matrix)

  • Process: Pyruvate is broken down into CO₂.
  • Key outputs: 2 ATP, NADH, and FADH₂
  • Analogy: Compare to a "power generator" producing electron carriers.

ELECTRON TRANSPORT CHAIN

(Inner Mitochondrial Membrane)

  • Process: NADH and FADH₂ donate electrons to produce ATP.
  • Key outputs: 32-34 ATP, H₂O as a byproduct
